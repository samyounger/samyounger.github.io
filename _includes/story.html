About Sam

Belief

I was drawn to web development with a belief that with the right drive technically anything could be done.

I have began my professional career in the City of London in February 2008 as a Stockbroker at Killik & Co. I transitioned from this after 5 years to Investment Management at Sarasin & Partners where I worked for a little under 4 years.

It was increasingly becoming clear to me that the financial services sector is transitioning rapidly to becoming more technically reliant. At this stage I was enjoying getting more stuck into learning Excel VBA and becoming more excited at the prospect of immense efficiency & productivity gains that development allows. It became clear to me that Excel has restrictions such as styling, and can be very temperamental, and so I researched alternative methods of development, of which Web Development appealed. I therefore began down a path to becoming a web developer in early 2015.

Code beginnings

I began learning to code in early 2015. From what began as years of learning and using Microsoft Excel, I realised I had accelerated past my peers in Excel capability, and the more I learnt the more I wanted to know about development. Web Developent was widely recommended on sites such as Quora and Stackoverflow as a great starting point to learn development. I came across a free online resource to start the learning on CodeCademy. I clearly remember the first evening I started to write HTML. I started at 18:00, and it was 1:00 in the morning before I realised it was well past my bed time. The thrill I got when my first text appeared in the web browser was great. Thus began my path to where I am today as a web developer.

General Assembly

I am not one to make rash decisions. It's all very well enjoying CodeCademy and playing around with direct instructions. I wanted to see just how far I had progressed, whether I had a knack for it, and if I would continue to enjoy coding in higher pressured circumstance. I signed up to the General Assembly Front End Web Development part-time course which takes place over 3 months. There I learnt more about how the internet works, and spent a lot of time learning and improving my HMTL, CSS/SASS, CSS frameworks, and has a small amount of dabbling in JavaScript/jQuery, and WordPress.

I was so upset when the GA course above finished, that I knew I had to pursue the coding path I was on. I knew how to make a basic front-end static website, but I was hungry to know more and be able to set up a full-stack website with all the functionality I could think of. I accelerated my learning in development by attending the General Assembly 3 month Web Development Immersive program in August 2016. It was a fantastic time in my life, I made great friends, produced some websites I'm really proud of, and it significantly improved my understanding of code and my ability to learn new languages and concepts. I now feel ready to take on any new learning challenge.
